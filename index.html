Science Overview

A unified model of the ocean — and everything it can help us learn.

We're building one shared model of the coastal ocean — how its physics, weather, runoff, waves, tides, and life all connect — so it can help us learn what's happening beneath the surface and what's coming next. This snapshot shows what we've tested, what held up under audit, and what still needs stronger proof: every claim tied to real evidence, with the failures kept on the record, right beside the wins.

The network, by place

Pick a dataset, then a view. Coverage = where the monitoring sites are and how often they go over the limit. Model skill (beach bacteria only) colors each site by the model's actual held-out ranking skill (AUC) there; sites with too few held-out events stay hollow. We never paint confidence where there's no support, and never interpolate a surface over open water.

Other basins (e.g. Japan / Tokyo Bay) are held under a research-only license, so their station data isn't republished here.

What kind of thing is this?

The lab is careful about words. An untrained XGBoost or neural net is a learner, not a model — it only becomes a trained model once it's fitted, evaluated, and registered. A baseline is an honest comparator, not a discovery. A histogram is a lens. This is the working vocabulary, with real examples from the registry and signal catalog.

How to read this site

Results are graded honestly: Supported held up under audit, Caveated is real but limited, Null was tested and didn't hold. Signals carry the catalog verdicts (PRIMARY/KEEP kept, WASH no lift, REJECT hurt, UNTESTED not yet gated). Nulls and failures are kept on the record beside the wins. The map shows observation coverage, not an extrapolated prediction surface.
