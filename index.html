Monterey Bay AI Lab

Can AI tell you which days the coast is unsafe?

Beaches too dirty to swim. Shellfish too toxic to eat. Water too low on oxygen. Lakes choked with algae. This is the lab's honest scorecard for forecasting those rare days. We test whether machine-learning models — fed weather, river, and ocean data — actually beat the simplest possible guess: "today will look a lot like yesterday." Usually they don't, so we keep the dead ends on the record right beside the wins — and show the data behind every claim. Pick a thread below.

How to read this site

Result grades

Supported
Held up under audit — beat a real baseline on held-out data, with confidence intervals.
Caveated
A real signal, but with limits you should read (narrow slice, soft generalization, screening-only).
Null
Tested and did not clear the bar. Not hidden — kept visible so it isn't re-tried blindly.
Exploratory
An open lane; correlations exist but haven't survived strict cross-validation yet.

Signal verdicts (what a feature did for a target)

PRIMARY / KEEP
Added real, audited skill over the baseline.
WASH
No lift once the baseline was fair.
REJECT
Hurt the model.
UNTESTED
Not yet gated.

The numbers

AUC
Ranking skill: 0.5 ≈ random, 1.0 = perfect ordering of event vs non-event days.
AP
Average precision — for rare events, how high real events rank. ΔAP is the gain over a baseline.
Baseline
The honest comparator a model must beat — usually persistence (yesterday) or seasonal climatology, not a strawman.
Held-out / perm-null
Test rows kept out of fitting; a permutation null checks the lift isn't luck.

The network, by place

Pick a dataset, then a view. Coverage = where monitoring sites are and how often they go over the limit. Model skill (beach bacteria only) colors each site by actual held-out ranking skill (AUC). Evidence grid groups nearby sites into map cells so you can see observation support and model confidence together. Other basins (e.g. Japan / Tokyo Bay) are held under a research-only license, so their station data isn't republished here.

Glossary

This lab is careful about words. An untrained XGBoost or neural net is a learner, not a model — it only becomes a trained model once it's fitted, evaluated, and registered. A baseline is an honest comparator, not a discovery; a signal carries verdicts (KEEP / WASH / REJECT) per target. Each term below links to its real examples from the registry and signal catalog.

About this snapshot

A public science snapshot from the Monterey Bay AI Lab. Every result is graded honestly — Supported held up under audit, Caveated is real but limited, Null was tested and didn't hold — and nulls stay on the record beside the wins. The map shows observation coverage, never an extrapolated prediction surface. Downloads and reproduction live under Data & reproduce.
