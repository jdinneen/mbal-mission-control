Which lake gets help first?

Harmful algae blooms turn lakes green and toxic. You can't watch every lake, and you can't treat every lake. This is how a free satellite helps a manager decide where to send the crew this week — and an honest look at whether we can call a bloom before it happens.

Big text — made for an 8th grader Mono line — every number for a data scientist
01 · The problem
1 / 8
Algae blooms can poison a lake. But there are thousands of lakes and only a few crews to send.
TARGET: freshwater cyanobacteria (harmful algal blooms). DECISION: allocate scarce sampling/treatment crews across a whole portfolio of water bodies.
02 · Today's tools
2 / 8
Today's tools are great — but each one watches a single spot, or a single water sample.
Competitors: LG Sonic MPC-Buoy (in-situ phycocyanin sensor + ultrasound, one buoy per body), In-Situ (sensors), FlowCam (image analysis of grab samples). Hardware/instrument-led; no cross-water-body prioritisation.
03 · Our idea
3 / 8
Our idea: use a free satellite that already photographs every lake on Earth.
Signal = Sentinel-2 NDCI = (B5−B4)/(B5+B4), a water-masked red-edge chlorophyll index, 10–20 m resolution. Free, whole-portfolio, resolves small bodies the 300 m CyAN product can't.
04 · Score every lake
4 / 8
The satellite gives every lake a score. Red lakes look like a bad, toxic bloom.
Validated against lab microcystin: within-lake AUC 0.80 (n=30 pairs); independent CA FHABS test AUC 0.688 (130 confirmed blooms vs 130 reported-but-tested-clean look-alikes). Severe-only: blind to low/moderate.
05 · Make the list
5 / 8
We rank the lakes and send crews to the top of the list — instead of just "the lakes that went bad last year."
Baseline to beat = the recurrence-placement heuristic (rank by past advisories) — this is effectively where you'd place scarce buoys. Metric = capture@budget (severe blooms caught in the top-k% of crews sent).
06 · Does it work?
6 / 8
It works: our list catches more bad blooms than the old way — and two independent critics checked it and agreed.
NDCI − recurrence = +0.083 capture@15%, water-body-clustered CI [0.017, 0.155], perm p=0.024; +0.062 [0.004, 0.119] even giving recurrence its strongest hand. Two default-refute adversaries (code + write-up) approved; nothing killed. Honest bound: single study, n=260; it ranks, it isn't a high-recall detector (top-15% still misses ~75%).
07 · Forecasting
7 / 8
Can we call a bloom before it happens? Mostly no — the future looks a lot like right now. Only in the Northeast & Midwest do free weather forecasts buy a small head start.
General next-week target is at the persistence+recurrence ceiling: a skillful forecast adds +0.006 AP (lever +0.002) — no headroom for anyone, incl LG Sonic. Onset slice: +0.022 AP, carried by 1-week temperature/radiation forecasts (+0.0089 [0.0038,0.0141]; wind/precip null); holds NE +0.041 / MW +0.015 but washes in South & West.
08 · Bottom line
8 / 8
Bottom line: a free satellite helps you send crews to the right lakes first — cheaper and everywhere, not one buoy at a time.
Promoted to the signals catalog as ndci_severe_bloom → cyano_control_targeting (PRIMARY). Not a sensor-skill win over a buoy on a body it monitors; the win is coverage + cost + a real targeting edge.

The write-up · both audiences

The same story, two depths

Each question is answered first in plain words, then — open the grey drawer — with every number a data scientist needs.

● GOAL 1 · TARGETING — WIN (small, single-study)
Q1Where should the crew go this week?
You can't visit every lake, so you need a good to-do list. The old list is "watch the lakes that went bad before." Our list scores every lake from a free satellite photo and puts the ones that look toxic on top. When we tested both lists against real lab results, our list caught more of the truly bad lakes for the same number of crews. It's not magic — it misses plenty, and it's one study — but it beats the old way, works on lakes nobody is watching, and costs nothing.
For the data scientist
Held-out unit = water body (120 distinct), water-body-clustered bootstrap CI. Test = CA FHABS, n=260 (130 advisory-confirmed severe blooms vs 130 reported-but-tested-clean hard negatives). Score = Sentinel-2 NDCI severe-bloom index (previously in-situ validated vs lab microcystin: within-lake AUC 0.80, n=30; independent FHABS AUC 0.688 reproduced here).

● GOAL 2 · FORECASTING — ceiling, with a small scoped edge
Q2Can we predict the bloom before it happens?
Everyone wants a crystal ball. The honest answer: for most lakes, next week looks like this week, so "just assume it keeps doing what it's doing" is already hard to beat — and no company beats it, including the expensive buoy. The one place a free weather forecast helps is spotting a currently-clean lake about to bloom in the Northeast & Midwest, using the temperature and sunlight forecast. In the South and West, even that edge disappears.
For the data scientist
Panel = EPA CyAN weekly × 481 lakes joined to daily open-meteo met; target = high/very_high bloom at week t+1; forward split train ≤2023 / test ≥2024; fair baseline = 8-feature persistence+recurrence +season HGBT (AP 0.776 ≫ best single column 0.705).
  • General target: at the ceiling — weather model +0.006 AP over the fair baseline; forecast lever +0.002. No skill headroom for anyone incl LG Sonic (persistence is physics).
  • Onset slice (currently-clean → bloom next week): +0.022 AP over the fair recurrence model [0.0135, 0.0305]; forecast lever +0.008 [0.0026, 0.0136].
  • Committed decomposition: carried by 1-week temp/radiation forecast (+0.0089 [0.0038, 0.0141]); wind/precip forecast null (+0.0007 [−0.0038, 0.0052]) — i.e. the genuinely-forecastable free variables.
  • Region transfer (LORO vs fair baseline): NE +0.041 [0.025,0.059], MW +0.015 [0.003,0.027] (CI excl 0); South −0.001, West −0.001 (CI incl 0). NE/Midwest-scoped, not universal.
Q3How do we know it isn't wishful thinking?
Every claim here had to survive a critic whose job was to prove it wrong. One critic re-ran all the code from scratch; a second critic checked that the words matched the numbers and caught a claim we hadn't actually computed yet — so we computed it for real before saying it. What you read is only what survived both.
For the data scientist
Two independent default-refute adversaries. #1 reproduced both scripts exactly, ran 5-seed sensitivity, gave recurrence its strongest un-de-leaked hand, decomposed the forecast lever → APPROVE-BUT-SCOPE, nothing killed. #2 audited the write-up vs artifacts: found the temp/rad-vs-wind/precip decomposition was asserted but not committed → we added it as a committed, tested run; reframed "LG Sonic ≡ recurrence" as a coverage/cost claim; added the operating-point sensitivity; regenerated a stale JSON. All 13 unit tests pass; promoted to signals/catalog.yaml (validator + 11 catalog tests green).
Q4Who is this actually for?
A water utility or state agency that looks after many lakes and reservoirs but only has a couple of field crews. Each week they get a free, whole-map priority list of which water bodies to sample, treat, or post warning signs at first — including small or unwatched lakes a buoy fleet could never afford to cover. In the Northeast & Midwest they also get a small early heads-up before a clean lake turns.
