Verified

On 25 years of real closures across 28 Irish production areas, a duration-based trigger tracks actual lost-harvest weeks with a correlation of 0.77. Tightening the trigger from “pay on any closure” to “pay on ≥3 consecutive closed weeks” cuts unjustified payouts from 33% to ~1% while still catching 70% of material-loss years. The dial below is live.

Parametric insurance pays when a measured trigger fires, not after a claims investigation. That speed is the whole value — and its whole risk. If the trigger fires when the farmer didn’t really lose money, the insurer overpays; if it stays silent through a real closure, the policy failed the customer. The gap between “trigger fired” and “money was actually lost” is basis risk. This tool measures it on real data. Move the controls; every number and the heatmap recompute instantly.

Honest limits

  • This is proof-of-method, not your book. Irish public data is the worked example; the same engine runs on your portfolio’s regions, species and loss records.
  • Weeks with no sample aren’t assumed open. Closure is measured only where monitoring exists; sparse weeks are shown as “not monitored,” never silently counted.
  • Closed-weeks is an opportunity-loss proxy, not audited revenue. Real basis-risk pricing joins your indemnity and landings data.
  • No forecasting is claimed here. This verifies a trigger’s historical behaviour — it does not predict blooms. Skill claims would be tested separately against a persistence baseline.
